Project Deliverables

My deliverables for the project included thorough research, wireframing for the reports page, and creating the final design for the reports page. The research phase laid the foundation by identifying user needs and gathering insights. Using this information, I created wireframes to map out the structure, layout, and user flow of the reports page, ensuring it would meet both user and business requirements while fitting the design of the other pages of the web app, which were designed by other members in my team. Finally, I developed the polished, final design for the reports page, combining usability, aesthetics, and functionality to deliver a cohesive and user-friendly solution. These deliverables showcase my ability to carry a concept through from initial research to a fully realized design.



Wireframing is a crucial step in the design process where a basic layout or blueprint of a webpage, app, or project is created. It focuses on structure and functionality, outlining where key elements like navigation, content, and buttons will go, without getting into visual details like colors or images. Wireframes help teams visualize the flow and user experience early on, allowing for feedback and revisions before moving to more detailed designs. This makes wireframing an efficient way to plan and refine ideas.



Designing the final design for a prototype is about refining the initial ideas into a polished and fully-realized visual representation. It combines functionality, usability, and aesthetics to create an interface that aligns with user needs and project goals. The final design incorporates feedback from earlier stages, such as wireframing, and ensures all elements are cohesive and consistent. This stage is critical as it sets the standard for development, acting as the blueprint for the finished product while showcasing how the prototype will look and feel in its final form.
