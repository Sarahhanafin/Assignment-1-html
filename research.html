Research Journey

Before we could start think about designing the web app, we had to think about the user. In this case the users were the sales people and the people in the office (the Admin). To try and understand them we created Personas, User stories, Empathy maps, and then once we had done that we brainstormed features that our web app coupld possibly have for both the sales and admin to help both.


Admin Persona

Creating a persona for someone working in the office (the admin) helps us better understand their needs, goals, and challenges. It allows us to design solutions, such as tools or workflows, that are tailored to their specific role and responsibilities. A persona also ensures that we keep the user at the center of decision-making, making sure that any system we develop actually supports their work and make their day-to-day tasks easier. It gives a clear picture of who they are and what they value, fostering more effective communication and collaboration.

Sales Persona

Creating a persona for a salesperson helps us understand their daily responsibilities, challenges, and goals. It ensures that any tools or system we design, like a web app, are made to fit their specific needs, such as tracking tasks, logging sales, and managing client relationships. By defining a clear persona, we can focus on making their work easier, more efficient, and more impactful. It also helps us empathize with their role, making sure we provide solutions that will support their success and align with their workflow.

Admin Userstory

We create a user story for a person working in the office (the Admin) to understand their specific tasks, challenges, and goals. It helps us identify the tools and processes they need to stay organized and efficient, like managing reports and seeing their tasks. A user story keeps the focus on creating solutions that support their role, ensuring that new systems or improvements will actually align with their workflow. It also helps developers and designers empathize with their day-to-day experiences, making sure the end result truly meets their needs.

Sales Userstory

We create a user story for a salesperson to understand their needs, goals, and challenges from their perspective. It helps us clearly define what they need to accomplish, like tracking tasks, managing leads, or viewing sales reports, and why those features are important for their role. A user story also guides the design and development process by ensuring that solutions are practical, user-friendly, and directly aligned with their daily workflow. It ensures we're building and designing things that help their success and make their job more efficient.


Admin Empathy Map

We create an empathy map for a person working in the office to gain a deeper understanding of their thoughts, feelings, and challenges. It helps us step into their shoes and consider what they think, feel, say, and do in their role. By mapping their experiences, we can identify pain points and opportunities for improvement, ensuring that solutions we design truly address their needs. An empathy map fosters user-centered design and helps create tools or processes that make their work smoother and more effective.

Sales Empathy Map

We create an empathy map for a salesperson to better understand their daily experiences, motivations, and challenges. By exploring what they say, do, think, and feel, we can gain valuable insights into their perspective and identify areas where they might struggle or need support. This helps us design tools or processes that align with their needs, such as simplifying task tracking or enhancing client management. An empathy map ensures we remain focused on creating solutions that make their work more effective, rewarding, and less stressful.

Admin Brainstorm

Brainstorming the main features of a web app for office workers helps us identify the specific tools they need to stay organized and efficient. It allows us to explore ideas for features like task management, contacts, and report reviews that align with their daily responsibilities. This collaborative process ensures we consider various perspectives, leading to a well-rounded app that meets their needs. By prioritizing key features through brainstorming, we can create a solution that truly supports their work and simplifies office operations.

Sales Brainstorm

Brainstorming the main features of a web app designed for salespeople helps us identify their core needs and prioritize functionality. It allows us to generate creative ideas for tools that will make their work easier, like task tracking, sales logging, and reporting. By collaborating in a brainstorming session, we ensure that all perspectives are considered, leading to a well-rounded app that aligns with their workflow. This process sets the foundation for designing a user-friendly, effective solution that meets their everyday challenges.
